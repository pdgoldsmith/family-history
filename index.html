The Goldsmith Family

A documented genealogy of Paul David Goldsmith's paternal line — from the towns of the Austro-Hungarian Empire and Russian Pale of Settlement to New York City and beyond.

This site presents what is actually known — and what is genuinely uncertain — about the Goldsmith family's origins in Eastern Europe. Every factual claim is cited to a primary source. Where records conflict or family tradition diverges from the documentary evidence, that is stated plainly. Where a conclusion is probable rather than proven, it is described that way.

Work in Progress
Research is ongoing. This document will be updated as new records are found. All conclusions reflect the state of research as of March 2026.

Family Members

David Goldsmith
~1858 – October 13, 1941
Goldsmith and jeweler. Born in the Austro-Hungarian Empire; worked in Russian Kamianets-Podilskyi; immigrated to New York ~1895. The first Goldsmith in America.

About This Research

The research behind this site was conducted between 2024 and 2026 by Paul Goldsmith with the assistance of an AI research partner. Primary sources consulted include U.S. federal census records, California and New York vital records, ship passenger manifests (NARA microfilm series M237, T715, T840), Ukrainian state archive records (DAKhmO Fond 277; TsDIAL Fond 701), Polish national archive records (AGAD), and Dutch emigration registers. All records inspected are documented in a research log maintained alongside this project.

The family's European surname was Goldschmidt (confirmed by Lena's 1898 ship manifest and multiple U.S. documents). The Hebrew/Jewish surname used simultaneously was Berliner — confirmed from a 1882 rabbinical birth register in Kamianets-Podilskyi, Ukraine, which shows both names for the same individual in the same entry. The origin of the "Goldsmith" anglicisation on arrival in America is debated within the family and discussed in David's article.
