Why Bluma Matters to the Research

Bluma is the only one of David's siblings confirmed to have immigrated to America, and the records she left behind provide important corroboration of the family's origins. Three pieces of evidence in particular are significant:

  • Birthplace "Romania" (1930 Census): Bluma listed her birthplace as Romania — consistent with the post-1918 political reality of the Galician/Bukovinian border region where the family originated. See the "The Romanian" section in David's article.
  • "Bluma Cooperman" (Louis's death certificate): Her son Louis's death certificate records her as "Bluma Cooperman — Russia." Since her maiden name was Goldsmith (confirmed separately), "Cooperman" is a third independent corroboration of the Kipperman/Cooperman family name running through David's side of the family.
  • Gravestone Hebrew inscription: "בלומע בת ר אברהם" = Bluma daughter of Reb Abraham. Her father's name is given as Abraham — consistent with family oral tradition but in tension with the 1882 Kamianets birth register, which gives David's father as Shlomo. This discrepancy is discussed in David's article under "David's Father."
